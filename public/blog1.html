An Ultimate Guide to Wrap Skirts


A wrap skirt is a type of skirt that wraps around the waist and is fastened by overlapping front panels. Most of the time, it is secured with ties, snaps, or buttons to hold it in place. The wrap design of the skirt allows for adjustability, making it suitable for various body shapes and sizes. One of the best things about wrap skirts is its versatility. It's because it can be styled in different ways to create both casual and more formal looks.

Wrap skirts are a great staple for the spring and summer seasons. It is easy to style and pull off, too. If you are thinking of trying to wear a wrap skirt but need some ideas on how to style them, we are here to help you. Whether you're a fashion enthusiast looking to refresh your wardrobe or a newcomer to the world of wrap skirts, this article is here to inspire and empower you. We are going to give you more information about the different wrap skirt designs along with the various creative outfit combinations that you can try. Therefore, read on as we're giving you the ultimate guide to wrap skirts.

Did you know that wrap skirts have a rich history that can be traced back to ancient civilizations? It's because the concept of wrapping fabric around the body to create a skirt-like garment has been prevalent in different cultures around the world.

In ancient times, wrap skirts have roots in ancient civilizations like Egypt, Greece, and Mesopotamia. In these cultures, both men and women would wear simple-wrapped garments around their lower bodies for practicality and modesty. In Southeast Asia, a traditional garment called the sarong was invented. It is a type of wrap skirt that has been worn for centuries. It is usually made of lightweight fabric cotton or silk and is wrapped and tied around the waist.

There are also many cultures in the world that have their own versions of wrap skirts as part of their traditional attire. For instance, the Scottish kilt worn by men is also a type of wrap skirt that is made of tartan fabric. In the early 20th century, wrap skirts became popular as a symbol of women's liberation and the rejection of restrictive clothing. In the 1940s, fashion designer Claire McCardell introduced the “popover” wrap skirt, which was a wrap-style garment worn as a skirt or a dress.

But the wrap skirt that we know today became popular in the 1970s with the iconic designs of Diane von Furstenberg, a Belgian-born fashion designer. In 1974, she introduced her signature wrap dress which became a fashion sensation and symbolized female empowerment and freedom of movement. Since then, wrap skirts have remained a fashion staple, evolving in style, length, and fabric choices. They continue to be loved for their versatility, flattering fit, and ease of wear. From casual everyday wear to elegant evening ensembles, wrap skirts have become a timeless and beloved garment in the fashion world.
